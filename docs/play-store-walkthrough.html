Privacy — Voice Notes disclosure

Voice Notes (optional) Voice Notes lets you capture a thought, memo, or meeting and turn it into a searchable note. It uses the microphone (requested only when you start recording) and keeps your data on your device: • Recording is on-device — the audio is written to app-private storage on your device. • Transcription is 100% on-device (Whisper); the audio is never uploaded to transcribe it. • The recording, its transcript, and the note live in a separate on-device database, excluded from cloud backup and device-to-device transfer, and never part of Candela’s optional cloud sync — Voice Notes stay only on the device that made them. • The AI summary is opt-in, per note: a note’s transcript text is sent to your own configured cloud AI provider (BYOK — Anthropic, OpenAI, or local Ollama) only when you explicitly tap Summarize on that note. Nothing is sent automatically, in the background, or while recording. No audio ever leaves your device. The only Voice Notes data that can leave is the text transcript of a single note, and only on your explicit Summarize tap. Uninstalling Candela deletes the notes database and every recording with it.
